Personal Loan Guide

Personal loans are unsecured — no collateral needed — which is why rates are higher than home or car loans. Current rates range from 10.49% (Axis, IndusInd for top credit profiles) to 36% (digital lenders, low CIBIL). Always check the processing fee: at 2–2.5%, it adds ₹6,000–7,500 to a ₹3 lakh loan.

What is the minimum CIBIL score for a personal loan?

Most banks require 700+. HDFC, ICICI, and Axis require 750+ for their lowest rates. A score below 650 will likely result in rejection or very high rates (18–24%).

Is it better to take a personal loan or use a credit card?

For amounts above ₹50,000 and if you need more than 3 months to repay, a personal loan is almost always cheaper. Credit card interest is 36–42% p.a. vs 10.5–14% for personal loans.

Can I prepay a personal loan early?

Yes, but most lenders charge a foreclosure penalty of 2–5% on outstanding amount after a lock-in period (usually 6–12 months). Check your loan agreement before prepaying.

What documents are needed for a personal loan?

Typically: PAN card, Aadhaar, last 3 months salary slips, 6 months bank statement, and Form 16. Self-employed need ITR for 2 years and business proof.
