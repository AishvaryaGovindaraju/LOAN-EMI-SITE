What EMI-to-income ratio do banks use?

Most Indian banks use 40–50% of monthly take-home (net) salary. If your total existing EMIs + new EMI exceed this, the loan gets rejected. 40% is the safe zone; 50% is the absolute maximum most lenders allow.

Does salary mean gross or take-home?

Banks assess take-home (net) salary — after PF, tax, and other deductions. Use your in-hand salary, not CTC.

Can I increase my loan eligibility?

Yes: (1) Add a co-applicant with income, (2) Close existing loans before applying, (3) Increase tenure — longer tenure = lower EMI = higher eligibility, (4) Improve CIBIL score to get a lower rate.

Why is my actual loan offer lower than this calculator shows?

Banks also factor in CIBIL score, employment type, company profile, age, and credit history. This calculator uses only the income-based formula. Treat it as the upper ceiling.
