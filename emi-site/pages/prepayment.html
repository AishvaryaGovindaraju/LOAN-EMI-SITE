Prepayment FAQs

When is the best time to prepay a loan?

Early in the tenure — when most of your EMI is going toward interest. Prepaying in the first 30–40% of the tenure gives the maximum benefit since interest is front-loaded in reducing-balance loans.

Do banks charge a penalty for prepayment?

For floating rate home loans, RBI mandates zero foreclosure charges. For fixed rate loans and personal loans, banks may charge 2–4% of the outstanding amount. Check your loan agreement.

Should I invest the extra money or prepay the loan?

If your loan rate is above 10% (personal loan), prepaying is usually better. For home loans at 8–9%, if you can earn 12%+ returns (equity SIP), investing may be better. But prepayment is risk-free — investment returns are not guaranteed.

Is it better to reduce EMI or tenure when prepaying?

Reducing tenure saves more total interest. Reducing EMI improves monthly cash flow. Most financial advisors recommend reducing tenure for maximum savings.
