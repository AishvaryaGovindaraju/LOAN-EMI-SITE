Home Loan EMI Guide

For a ₹50 lakh home loan at 8.5% for 20 years, the monthly EMI is approximately ₹43,391. Over 20 years, your total outgo is ₹1.04 crore — you pay ₹54 lakh extra as interest. This is why choosing the right rate and tenure matters so much.

SBI and PNB currently offer the lowest home loan rates starting at 8.50% p.a. Use the quick-fill bank buttons above to instantly calculate EMI at any bank's rate. Always factor in the processing fee (0.25–1%) and GST on it.

What salary do I need for a ₹50 lakh home loan?

Banks allow EMI up to 40–50% of take-home pay. For EMI of ₹43,391 (₹50L, 8.5%, 20 yr), you need a take-home of at least ₹87,000–₹1,08,000/month.

Can I get a home loan with CIBIL score 700?

Most banks prefer 750+. A score of 700–749 may get approval at 0.25–0.5% higher rate. Below 700 makes approval unlikely — improve your score first.

What is the maximum home loan tenure in India?

Most banks offer up to 30 years. SBI and HDFC go up to 30 years for salaried applicants. Tenure usually cannot extend beyond your retirement age (60–65).

Is home loan interest tax deductible in India?

Yes. Under Section 24(b), interest up to ₹2 lakh/year is deductible for self-occupied property. Under Section 80C, principal repayment up to ₹1.5 lakh is deductible.
